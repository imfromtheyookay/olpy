Call Grades
Call grades are used to identify the call urgency, and response time.
›
Used to identify suspects based on appearance/ethnicity.
IC1
White-skinned European
English, Scottish, Welsh, French, German, Swedish, Norwegian, Polish, Russian
IC2
Dark-skinned European
Greek, Cypriot, Turkish, Sicilian, Sardinian, Spanish, Italian
IC3
African-Caribbean
West Indian, Guyanese, Black African (including those with light complexion)
IC4
South Asian
Indian, Pakistani, Bangladeshi or Sri Lankan
IC5
South/East Asian
Malaysian, Burmese, Filipino, Mongolian
IC6
Arabian/North African
Algerian, Tunisian, Moroccan and North African
IC0 / IC7 / IC9
Unknown
Origin/appearance not determined
Call grades identify the urgency of a call and the expected response time.
I
Immediate
The immediate presence of a police officer will have a significant impact on the outcome. Typically where there is, or is likely to be, a danger to life, serious threat of violence, serious damage to property or serious injury.
⏱ 15 MINUTES
🚨 SIRENS & ELS
S
Significant
There is a degree of importance or urgency associated with the initial police action, but an emergency response is not required.
⏱ 60 MINUTES
🔵 ELS ONLY
E
Extended
Police attendance is required, but an emergency response is not. No lights or sirens needed.
⏱ 48 HOURS
🟢 NO ELS OR SIRENS
R
Referred
Police attendance is not required. The caller has been dealt with appropriately by the call handler.
⛔ NO RESPONSE NEEDED
